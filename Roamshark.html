Roamsharks are very good predators that are skilled in hunting both underwater and on land. They're the most common threat for small fishing villages.

Contents
  1. Appearance
  2. Behavior
  3. Past
  4. Variants and Related Lifeforms

Appearance

These small predators are equipped with both limbs to run on land and fins to help them swim swiftly. They use their teal coloring to camoflauge in the water and ambush their prey. Many Roamsharks posses scars they get from fighting with others of their kind.

Behavior

Their method of hunting differs when they lurk on fish and when they try to hunt something bigger above the water. While in water, they try to outswim the fishes and bite them by their rear fins to weaken them and later to swallow whole. When it comes to hunting on land, they hide themselves on the shore and wait for their prey to come close enough to swiftly drag them to the water, where they'll be helpless. Roamsharks are a very competitive creatures and will even fight eachother when it comes to claiming their food reward.

Past

Roamsharks predecessors are known to live deep underwater and usually be even thrice as big as them now. Their size reduction is most likely a way to make them more agile and less noticeable.

Variants and Related Lifeforms

The remains of Roamshark's predecessors can be found deep underwater in lakes and seas. They got named Ancient Roamsharks, since the age of the skeletons spans centuries.
